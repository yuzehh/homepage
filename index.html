About Me

I am currently a postdoctoral fellow in the Computer Science Department (CSD) at Carnegie Mellon University, co-advised by Prof. Peter Steenkiste and Prof. Srinivasan Seshan. I received my Ph.D. from the AIoT Lab at The Chinese University of Hong Kong, advised by Prof. Guoliang Xing, and my B.Eng. with honors in Electrical Engineering from Zhejiang University.

My research builds intelligent infrastructures that enhance how mobile systems perceive, learn, and adapt to the physical world. I explore how stationary infrastructure nodes (e.g., smart roadside lampposts or indoor sensing units), mobile devices (e.g., autonomous vehicles, AR glasses, and indoor robots), and edge servers jointly develop collective perception and intelligence under constraints of compute, bandwidth, latency, and energy through system-level co-design of sensing, networking, and machine learning.

My work has been recognized with multiple Best Paper and Demo Awards at ACM MobiCom, and selection as an MIT EECS Rising Star, among other honors from both academia and industry.
